How did you get your first job in Computer Science?

Ans: I took as many computer science classes as I could at Stanford where I was a math undergraduate. I interviewed with IBM on the campus and was offered a job in June 1965 in Los Angeles.

 

What was the aforementioned job?

Ans: Systems Engineer for the QUIKTRAN interactive Fortran programming service. You entered your programs online and then executed them via a time sharing service.

 

Did you always want to be a Computer Scientist?

Ans: No I started out wanting to be a particle physicist but quickly came to like math and computer science. Then my graduate years at UCLA draw me into computer networking.

Is high school too late a time to research colleges?

Ans: no, but I would start in 8th grade.

 

What Got You interested in Computer Science?

Ans: I got to program a Bendix G-15 paper tape computer at UCLA while I was in high school (1960) and became fascinated by the idea that you could program your own universe and it would do what you told it to do!

 

What does your job look like?

Ans: See Wikipedia for Vinton Cerf. I am a standards, policy and technical architecture person now. I travel about 80% of my time around the world seeking to get more Internet built. I sit on a number of advisory boards for the USG and other non-US organizations.

 

Do you have any advice for someone pursuing Computer Science?

Ans: Yes -find something you are good at and you like doing. Try to find a job that capitalizes on these traits. The field is wide open. Don't drink too much AI kool-aid. Join ACM, Read CACM.
